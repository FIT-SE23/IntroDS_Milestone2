\opening{Dear Editor-in-Chief,}

On behalf of the authors, I am happy to submit our paper titled "Premonition Net, A Multi-Timeline Transformer Network Architecture Towards Strawberry Tabletop Yield Forecasting" at Computers and Electronics in Agriculture for your consideration.

Our paper shows how transformer neural networks can be used to ingest multiple timelines, the past, present, and premonitions of the future, to attain excellent forecasting performance. This follows how growers currently attempt to forecast their crops here in the UK, by using either simple heat hours models or previous year averages. In our collaboration with one of the UKs largest soft-and-stone fruit producers we have seen how current predictive performance varies wildly from site-to-site upwards of 15\% forecasting accuracy, 3 weeks ahead. However our multi-timeline-transformer (MTT) achieves a loss of ~8\% on our strawberry tabletop site, also forecasting 3 weeks ahead, over recent years.

Our paper proposes variations on standard approaches, to make it possible to ingest multiple timelines, with various sampling rates. All while creating a neural network architecture that can accurately predict on them, to take into considerations the pasts relation to the present, the presents deviation from the norm, and the premonition which serves to mitigate optimal yield outcomes.

\closing{Sincerely,}
